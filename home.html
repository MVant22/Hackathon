Linkin Park, formed in 1996 in Agoura Hills, California, rose to global fame with their debut album *Hybrid Theory* (2000). Their signature blend of nu-metal, alternative rock, and electronic elements, combined with powerful lyrics and dynamic performances, made them one of the most influential bands of the 21st century.
interesting anecdotes on the band

The Band's Original Name Was "Xero"

Before becoming Linkin Park, the band was originally called Xero and later changed to Hybrid Theory before finally settling on Linkin Park.

The name was inspired by Lincoln Park in Santa Monica, California.

Chester Bennington Auditioned by Recording Over a Demo

When Linkin Park was searching for a new vocalist, Chester Bennington stood out by recording vocals over their instrumental demos and submitting them as his audition.

His performance was so impressive that he was immediately invited to join the band.

Their Music Helped People Through Hard Times

Chester Bennington and the band often received letters from fans who said their music helped them cope with depression, anxiety, and personal struggles.

The band always appreciated this, knowing their music had a deep emotional impact.
