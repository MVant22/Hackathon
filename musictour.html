Projekt Revolution

One of Linkin Park's most iconic and famous tours was "Projekt Revolution," a touring festival that mixed rock, metal, and hip-hop. The band headlined this multi-artist event, which took place across North America and Europe between 2002 and 2008.

Meteora World Tour

The Meteora World Tour was one of Linkin Park's most successful and electrifying tours, supporting their second studio album, "Meteora" (2003). The tour spanned from 2003 to 2004, covering North America, Europe, Asia, and Australia.

During this tour, Linkin Park played a free concert in Mexico City (2012) for over 50,000 fans, celebrating the success of Living Things!
A Thousand Suns World Tour

The A Thousand Suns World Tour was launched to promote Linkin Park's fourth studio album, A Thousand Suns (2010). This tour marked a shift in the band's musical style, blending electronic, rock, and industrial elements.

During this tour, Linkin Park played their first-ever concert in Abu Dhabi (United Arab Emirates) as part of the F1 Yasalam After-Race Concerts in 2010!
Living Things Tour (2012-2013) - A High-Energy Global Tour

The Living Things Tour was launched to promote Linkin Park's fifth studio album, Living Things (2012). This tour was known for its high-energy performances, electronic-rock fusion, and fan-favorite setlists.

During this tour, Linkin Park played a free concert in Mexico City (2012) for over 50,000 fans, celebrating the success of Living Things!
The Hunting Party Tour (2014-2015) - Linkin Park's Return to Heavy Rock

The Hunting Party Tour was launched to promote Linkin Park's sixth studio album, The Hunting Party (2014). Unlike their previous electronic-heavy albums, this tour marked a return to their raw, aggressive rock sound.

During the Summer Sonic Festival (Japan, 2014), Linkin Park surprised fans by playing their entire "Hybrid Theory" album live from start to finish!
One More Light Tour (2017) - Linkin Park's Final Tour with Chester Bennington

The One More Light Tour was launched to promote Linkin Park's seventh studio album, One More Light (2017). This tour took a softer, more emotional approach, focusing on melodic, electronic, and pop-infused tracks rather than their signature heavy rock sound.

Linkin Park unknowingly played their last-ever show with Chester Bennington. The final song he performed live was "Bleed It Out."
